How to talk to a child from birth to 2 years

Parents' focus should never be on how many words a child can say. According to experts, "There is no need for parents to get tired of talking to their children all day. Setting a target to teach the child so many words per day is not the case. Instead, they should spend time on small, quality interactions. Linguists at Harvard Graduate School of Education and pediatricians at Boston Medical Center have jointly published guidelines on how to talk to babies between the ages of newborn and 2 years.

  • Exaggerate the manner of speaking:

    Babies usually do not respond when spoken to in a normal voice in their own voice. At this age, parents need to raise their voice, widen their eyes, make soft loving expressions, and look into the baby's eyes while talking. The same word or sentence may be repeated. Find out how your baby likes to talk and repeat it. Know that children's vision and hearing function slowly during this time. It gradually increases with age.

  • Respond to words or sounds:

    Respond to whatever sounds or noises the baby makes at this time the baby starts making different noises, parents must respond to these sounds. For example, if the child makes any noise while reciting the rhyme, then immediately open your eyes and ask with an expression, “Did you like the rhyme? Shall I say it again?” Say the rhyme again. Remember, children don't learn language by hearing the same words over and over on TV; Learning by interacting with people

  • Talk about what the child sees:

    According to experts, children of this age are quick to grasp the object they see in front of them when they say something about it. Talk to the child about something. For example, hold the story book in front of you and tell the child the name of what is seen in the picture. Introduce water at bath time, say what is being eaten at mealtime, name the toy that is being held while playing. Take it to the roof and show the bird and say in an excited voice that bird, the bird is flying.

  • Repetition of words:

    Repetition is the key to practicing any skill. Repeat what you are talking about when talking to the baby. For example, repeat the name of his favorite toy while playing.

  • Use gestures:

    Babies can understand gestures from 9 months. When you raise your hand and show him his favorite toy, he will know that he will play now. Pointing with the fingers is a very important practice to do with the baby. By pointing the child learns to recognize any object. Point with your fingers and then say the name of the object to the child and talk about it for a while with the child. Besides, the gestures that the child will learn at this time are - shaking the head means saying no, waving hands means patting etc. This early difference in the use of gestures plays a special role in children's acquisition of vocabulary skills.

  • Ask what, who, where national questions:

    At this level of conversation, the child's language skills increase greatly. Along with language skills, the child's memory and ability to learn new things also increases. Through questions, children learn how to initiate a conversation.

  • Use different words:

    This is the age to start collecting children's story books. Find a variety of books to introduce your child to new words. This is the time to familiarize the child with as many words as possible.
